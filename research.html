Age-Dependent Infection in Tribolium castaneum

Mentor: Dr. Tad Dallas   |   Department: Biological Sciences

Funding: Magellan Scholar Grant

This project investigated how host age influences infection outcomes with the fungal pathogen Beauveria bassiana in the red flour beetle. The research found that infection prevalence decreases with beetle age, while no significant differences were observed in time to infection. These findings contribute to understanding population-level disease dynamics and pest management strategies.

  • Hypotheses: Infection prevalence decreases with age; time to infection varies by age.
  • Findings: Supported hypothesis on prevalence; no distributional differences in infection timing.
  • Presented at: USC Undergraduate Research Symposium (April 2025)
